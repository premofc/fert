Input Guide & Project Details

Understand what each input means and why it matters. This helps you enter accurate values and interpret the recommendation with confidence.

Nitrogen (N)

Nitrogen supports green growth and chlorophyll formation. Low N often shows as pale/yellow leaves.

Phosphorus (P)

Phosphorus helps energy transfer and root development. It’s important for flowering and early growth.

Potassium (K)

Potassium improves stress tolerance, grain filling and overall crop strength.

Temperature

High temperature affects photosynthesis and respiration. It can change nutrient uptake and water loss.

Humidity

Very humid weather can increase disease risk and affect fertilizer timing.

Soil Moisture

Moisture affects nutrient movement and leaching. Too much moisture can wash fertilizer away.

Soil Type

Sandy drains quickly, clay holds water, loamy is balanced. Soil type changes how fertilizer behaves.

Output Label

The model predicts one fertilizer category (for example: Urea, DAP, NPK blends, etc.).

Tip

Use Auto Fetch for weather inputs, and enter N–P–K from your soil test for best accuracy.
